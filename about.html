The Public Journal explores the intersection between private and public, personal and community, documentation and literature. Through writing, it seeks to capture the rich and often overlooked facets of our unique experiences. Think of it as a textual celebration of self. In the past, entries have manifested themselves as journal entries, one-line confessions, bizarre reflections, love letters, prose, and a testicle X-ray from the doctor. All of them tell a true story, be it straightforward, elaborate, beautiful, or ugly.

Our issues collect together what has always been churned into private words and images: physicality, anxiety, and observations on the little poetic gestures that often go by unseen or at least unrecorded. Our duty is to make these intimately public.
